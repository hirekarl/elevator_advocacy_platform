## Engagement Email Draft

**Recipient:** Council Member Shekar Krishnan
**From:** Karl Johnson
**Subject:** Elevator accessibility in D26 buildings — a data tool for your housing work

---

Dear Council Member Krishnan,

My name is Karl Johnson. I'm a Queens resident and a fellow at Pursuit, the civic tech organization based in Long Island City, and I've been following your work on elevator accessibility closely — including the recent demonstration at the 7 train stations in Jackson Heights and Corona.

I want to share a tool that addresses the residential side of the same problem.

It's called **Elevator Advocate** ([elevatoradvocate.nyc](https://www.elevatoradvocate.nyc)) — a free platform that gives tenants a way to document elevator outages in their buildings, verify them through a two-report consensus mechanism, and take direct action: filing 311 complaints, contacting building management, or reaching your office. It's free, mobile-first, and available in English and Spanish.

The residents fighting for elevator access at 82nd Street and Jackson Heights are the same residents going home to rental buildings where the elevator is unreliable. For seniors, parents with strollers, and anyone with a mobility limitation, the problem doesn't end at the subway entrance.

I've completed a pilot audit of District 17 in the Bronx — analyzing 552 buildings against NYC Open Data — and I'd like to offer to run the same analysis for **District 26**. The methodology uses a dual-window filter: buildings with at least one complaint in the last 12 months and three or more over the past three years. This surfaces the chronic offenders rather than noise.

I'll be honest: this is a working prototype and the methodology hasn't yet had expert review. That's part of what I'm hoping conversations like this one can help address. The full source code and data analysis tools are publicly available for anyone on your team to examine: https://github.com/hirekarl/elevator_advocate

I'd welcome a 20-minute conversation with you or a member of your housing staff.

Thank you for the work you're doing for Queens residents.

In service,

**Karl Johnson**
Queens Resident | Pursuit AI-Native Fellow
[https://www.elevatoradvocate.nyc](https://www.elevatoradvocate.nyc)
