# MVP Pitch Scripts: Elevator Advocate
**Goal:** 2–3 Minute Direct-to-Camera Video
**Tone:** Conversational, Urgent, Resilient (The "Fellow New Yorker" voice)

---

## The Hook: The Vertical Desert (0:00 – 0:45)

**(Start: Close-up, direct eye contact. No background music, just a quiet Bronx street or a hallway if possible.)**

"Hi, I’m Karl Johnson. I live in District 17 in the Bronx. 

In my building, we have seniors who can't use the stairs. We have children who struggle going up and down eight flights of stairs to and from school. We have a neighbor with one leg who relies on a motorized wheelchair to get to the store.

But when our elevator breaks—which it does, constantly—my neighbors aren't just inconvenienced. They are oppressed. They are confined to their floors for days, weeks, and sometimes months on end.

Right now, their only hope is to 'hope and pray' that management feels like fixing it. Because the current system? Calling 311 and waiting for an out-of-date database to update? It’s high-friction, it’s slow, and it’s not saving anyone."

## The Pivot: Dignity Through Data (0:45 – 1:30)

**(Beat: Lean in slightly. Tone shifts from frustration to resolve.)**

"I’m a software engineer and an AI-Native fellow at Pursuit. And I realized that while I can't personally fix a broken motor, I *can* fix the information gap that lets management get away with this. 

I’m building **Elevator Advocate**. 

It’s simple: one button. When the elevator goes down, a tenant pushes a button. In real-time, every neighbor, every family member, and every care provider knows the status. 

But it’s more than just a status update. We are transforming these outages into **Loss of Service** metrics—hard, undeniable data that we can take into Housing Court or to the steps of City Hall. We’re moving from 'I think the elevator is broken' to 'This building has been 20% inaccessible for the last thirty days.'"

## The Ask: The Coalition (1:30 – 2:15)

**(Beat: Tonal shift to partnership. This is the 'Subtext' for the Councilmembers.)**

"To Justin Sanchez, Althea Stevens, and Chris Banks: I’m not just building an app. I’m building a tool for *you* to help *us*. 

I need your partnership to pilot this in District 17. We’ve identified the hotspots. We're ready to engage grassroots energy from partners like CASA and Mothers on the Move. What we need now is the legislative momentum to turn this data into accountability. 

This is my gift of service back to my community. I want to make sure that in a city as great as New York, accessibility isn't a luxury or a whim of a management company—it’s a fundamental right."

## The Closer: The Final Beat (2:15 – 2:30)

**(Beat: Soften the tone. A final personal appeal.)**

"My neighbors shouldn't have to pray for a working elevator. They should be able to rely on it. 

Let's build a future where no New Yorker is left behind on their own floor. 

I’m Karl Johnson. Let’s get to work."

---

## Practice Notes for Karl:
- **The "311" Beat:** When you mention 311, use a bit of 'New Yorker' frustration. Everyone knows that struggle.
- **The "One Button" Beat:** Use a hand gesture here to show simplicity. This appeals to the "Universal Accessibility" part of your mission.
- **The "Loss of Service" Beat:** Speak this clearly and firmly. This is the technical 'hook' for the Pursuit higher-ups and the 'legislative' hook for the reps.
